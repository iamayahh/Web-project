Types of Wax Used in Candle Making

There are several types of wax, each used for different purposes based on its characteristics. Below are the main types:

  • Paraffin Wax: The most common and widely used type in traditional candles. It is affordable, easy to color, and infuse with scents.
  • Beeswax: Derived from honeycombs, it has a light honey scent. It is natural, environmentally friendly, and burns cleanly and slowly.
  • Soy Wax: Made from soybean oil, it is an eco-friendly alternative to paraffin wax. It burns longer and is ideal for scented candles.
  • Coconut Wax: Extracted from coconut oil, it is another natural and eco-friendly option. It has a low melting point and works well with scents.
  • Gel Wax: Made from mineral oils, this wax is transparent and used to create visually appealing candles with embedded decorations like shells or stones.
  • Palm Wax: Derived from palm oil, it has a unique crystalline appearance that adds decorative patterns to candles.
